// => / /n => '' ### 2.1a - Balance sheet identity.\n\nBalance sheet identity, or accounting equation, is mathematical formula used to analyze the financial transactions that affects business. Such formula is as follows: \n$$\begin{aligned} \text{Assets} &= \text{Liabilities} +{\text{Equity}}\\[1pt] \end{aligned}$$\n\nThe amounts recorded by the entity must always balance. Therefore, assets should be equal to the sum of liabilities and equity.
